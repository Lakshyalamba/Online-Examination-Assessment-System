# Role And Scope

You own the evaluation and result lifecycle.

## What You Own

- objective grading behavior
- subjective review workflow
- review queue screens
- result aggregation and readiness transitions
- result publication controls
- student result detail view
- examiner result overview for owned exams

## What You Do Not Own

- question authoring
- exam creation and scheduling
- student attempt runtime screens
- admin user management, audit log, or system-wide analytics ownership

## Eight-Prompt Commit Map

1. evaluation contracts, strategy interfaces, and result-state helpers
2. objective grading implementations
3. result aggregation and readiness transitions
4. pending review queue listing
5. grading workspace and marks entry
6. review completion and publication gating
7. examiner result overview and publish actions
8. student result detail and final result-flow polish

## Success Criteria

- objective question types are graded correctly
- subjective answers can be reviewed and marked
- results only become visible after readiness and publication rules are satisfied
- pages match the calm, data-clear design language from `design.md`
